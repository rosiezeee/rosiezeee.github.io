NOT SO ITSY BITSY MOTHY

by Anonymous

When I was four or five years old, my mom and I were in the kitchen after dark. We heard a fluttering noise by the fridge, and realize that a moth had gotten into the house. Not just any moth though—one of those gigantic moths with the fake eyes on their wings. We started laughing at the magnitude of this moth, trying to chase it out of the house with a towel, when finally we herded it over to the door. Suddenly, everything changed in the blink of an eye. I was outside, the moth followed. My mom shut the door behind us. Fear began to well inside me as I realized the door was held shut, and I was out here with the moth that Godzilla always fights. It fluttered around my head, searching for light to feed off of. I looked up and through my tears I saw my mom laughing at this entire ordeal. I was finally let into the house, but my fear of moths followed me into the house as well that night. To this day, I get the chills thinking about them, I get jumpy when I see them because of their unpredictable movements, and my mom denies ever keeping me out there with that beast. I don't think I'll ever look at a moth the same way as long as I live.
