MOLEHILLS

by Anna Slusser

Back in October I suffered an acute head trauma playing the sport that I love (roller derby). It was to the degree that I had to stay home, doing nothing for a month. I couldn't use the computer or even read a book. Which meant dropping my classes and taking a lot of time off from work. Since then my brain power seems to be about half strength-- I forget things constantly (like simply words during conversations) and have to spend about twice as much time when learning new things.

I used to have very little fear of failure and now I feel myself constantly anxious that I am going to fail at every little thing-- saying the wrong word, being late to class, having my project suck, getting so dizzy that I can't continue doing a presentation, etc. I know that it's a lot of pressure to put myself under, but I can't make it stop. My brain literally won't let me.

I'm so afraid that I'm not going to be able to get through my classes and graduate. Every little molehill looks like a mountain right now.
