TERRIBLE, HORRIBLE, NO GOOD, VERY BAD FEAR

by Anonymous

Fear and I go way back. I've been a perfectionist for as long as I can remember. You can't fail if you don't even start, right? The struggle with fear is probably the thing that has held me back more than anything else.

For a long time I was afraid that I wasn't good enough to be an "artist," so I worked boring office jobs for over a decade until the pain of not making art was greater than my fear. I slowly started making art on the side, and taking printmaking classes at a community college. The scariest thing I've ever done was leaving that stable, "good" job to return to school to finish my art degree. I had to leave it in stages, reducing my hours from 50 to 30 to 20 over a couple of years, until I finally quit. Telling my boss I was leaving was terrible. I felt like I was going to vomit, and my voice shook as I spoke to him. But after it was done, I felt like a giant weight had lifted, and I could do anything I wanted. Euphoria! I wish I had done that much sooner. I regret all of the years I wasted not working toward goals that are important to me.

Even now that I'm doing exactly the thing I wanted to do for years, fear plagues me. I worry that I'll never get a design job, or that I'll hate it, or that my skills aren't good enough, or I'm not creative enough, or that I'm too old. I worry a lot about my future. Day to day, fear sometimes gets in the way of making the things I want to make. I'm afraid what I make will be terrible, so it's easier to not start, or to wait until the last minute until I'm forced into action. I suspect this is a common problem, but it often feels like everyone else has it together, and I'm the only one who struggles with this.

Anything I've ever done that involved risk and fear has turned out great, but it's hard to remember that in the moments I'm experiencing fear. I think my work would be a lot better if I could tackle this problem, and I would probably be happier.
