I FEAR FIRE IN ALL FORMS

by Denise

On July 7, 1986 I was set on fire by my mom's boyfriend. I spent months in and out of the hospital, more surgeries than I can remember. It changed my life forever and started my addiction to drugs. When I told my mother, she didn't believe me, and I not only went home to this person, he became the care giver and was the one to change bandages and clean the wounds. I have scars on my neck, arm and chest where I was burned to bone.

How I overcame this.... Well I still am very uncomfortable around large fires...I don't think I will ever go camping. But I can start a small contolled fire in a fire place or grill if I have a large bucket of water or a fire extinguisher close by. Gas fire places no longer bother me. Mind you all of this has happened in the last couple years and started by turning on a gas fire place and then turning it off when I just couldn't handle it anymore. The next day I would do it for longer. Then when that was no longer so scary I went to a gas grill and did the same thing. Then a fireplace. Which was the hardest. I threw alot of water and my shop vac got alot of use. I still panic when I feel that it;s too big. Why did I do this you might ask? 1. That man who hurt me can't win. I can't let his actions dictate my life. He doesn't get that control. 2. My kids grew up knowing I didn't do fire. I can still see thier faces when I not only started one but had them make smores. His actions don't get to impact their lives.

Side note becuase everyone asks. The man who set me on fire died when I was 18. He was run over by a train. My mother never believed me, and I didn't tell anyone else for years. I got sober when I was 16 and have been sober since. I can light candles, I don't do it often. My kids always had candles to blow out on their birthday, even when doing so would give me panic attaks and nightmares.
